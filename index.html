Investment Rationale

‍Region: Why Bordeaux?

  • Strong demand dynamics with low availability of stock helping to support prices
  • Price weakness in H1 2020 mainly driven by trade participants reducing inventory of younger
  • Bordeaux is now in reverse, presenting a good entry point
  • Significant demand for older wines (pre 2000) resulting in price appreciation that is yet to be reflected in younger vintages
  • Temporary US tariff suspension under the Biden administration on french wine likely to support prices and help drive price appreciation
  • Post-COVID luxury asset consumption likely to increase demand for most well known regions such as Bordeaux

‍Producer: Why Chateau Mouton Rothschild?

  • Famous ‘First Growth’ located in Pauillac, Bordeaux
  • Exceptional brand equity. Chateau Mouton Rothschild is owned by the most well-known family in the wine market, the Rothschilds
  • Each year since 1945, a famous artist has created an original artwork for the label of Chateau Mouton Rothschild, making each vintage distinct in their own right in increasing value from collectible perspective

‍Vintage: Why 2016?

  • Exceptional grape harvest due to the heat, with vines producing small concentrated berries
  • The quality of the wine resulted in almost perfect review scores from the most prominent critics in the industry
  • Despite the impressive critic scores, prices have barely moved since release making this look very attractive on a relative value basis, with good price appreciation potential
  • Relatively tight bid/ask spread of c.6% with good liquidity - one of the most traded wines on the Liv-ex platform in 2020
